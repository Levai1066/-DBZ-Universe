Heroes

Goku is cheerful, pure-hearted, and driven by a love of battle and learning. His compassion often leads him to spare defeated enemies, believing in redemption through challenge.

Vegeta is defined by arrogance, competitiveness, and a fierce sense of Saiyan pride. Initially merciless and self-serving, he gradually develops empathy and loyalty, influenced by his love for Bulma and their children.

Goten’s character symbolizes the continuation of Goku’s legacy—a bridge between generations of Saiyans. His friendship and fusion with Trunks represent teamwork and youthful courage, while his early mastery of power underscores the evolving strength of hybrid Saiyans in the Dragon Ball universe.

Gohan’s potential drew the attention of Piccolo, who became both his harsh mentor and surrogate father figure. Under Piccolo’s tutelage, he endured grueling survival training that awakened his Saiyan resilience

Trunks inherits Saiyan combat instincts and human intellect. His techniques include ki manipulation, swordsmanship (notably the Brave Sword), and multiple Super Saiyan transformations.

Raditz’s arrival marks a turning point for the series—from martial-arts adventure to interstellar war. Discovering that Goku refuses to join him, Raditz kidnaps his nephew Gohan to force compliance.

Nappa is portrayed as brutal, overconfident, and destructive. He takes pleasure in chaos and battle, often mocking opponents.

Bardock possesses extraordinary combat skills and tactical awareness, rivaling elite Saiyans despite his low-class status.

Broly remains one of the franchise’s most beloved and enduring characters, inspiring adaptations across video games, merchandise, and spin-off media.
